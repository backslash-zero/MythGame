In a land parched by unyielding sun, not a drop of rain for seasons, a desperate soul sought the riverbed, now a cracked scar. Tripping on the blistered earth, the figure plunged into a hidden, meager pool. As the last bubble of air escaped their lungs, a miracle stirred. From the muddled mix of flesh, water, and dirt, a sapling sprouted. It grew, boughs stretching heavenward, roots entwining the fallen, a holy tree born of desperation and divine intervention.
