She climbed down the gnarled oak, toes seeking footholds, eyes fixed on the river below. The water's song was a siren's call, promising peace. She stepped off the final branch, surrendering to the current. It pulled her under, and she did not resist. Her breath left her, bubbles rising like fleeing ghosts. Her form wavered, flesh softening, boundaries dissolving. She became the river, its course her path, its voice her own.
