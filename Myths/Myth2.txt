Trapped in the coffee-scented hut, the man shivered as the ogre leered. "Meat for meat," it growled, offering a bloody haunch. Unable to discern beast from man, he tore off his own hand, trading for the ogre's offering. Night after night, the ogre deceived, the man shedding his flesh, his life ebbing. When only his heart remained, the ogre laughed, tossing a pig's heart, the man's heartbeat fading, betrayed and weak.
