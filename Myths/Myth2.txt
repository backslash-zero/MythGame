In the dim cave, the ogre and his cub huddled, the air thick with coffee, hiding the scent of flesh. The man, cunning, offered a deal, "Your flesh for mine." The ogre grinned, slicing a beast's haunch, passing it as his own. The man, undeterred, presented a boar's leg, claiming it his own thigh. Deception layered, they feasted, each believing they'd tricked the other. Yet, the man's ruse was deeper, his body a puzzle of beast parts, sewn together, a mockery of life, deceiving even the ogre's keen eyes.
