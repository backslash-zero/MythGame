In a world parched, the air wavered with heat, the riverbeds cracked and dry. Yet, in the evenings, frogs croaked full-throated, and snakes slithered, scales gleaming as if slick with water. "Illusion," the villagers whispered, eyes on the frogs and snakes. "They've stolen our rain." A brave few followed the creatures, finding them disappearing into a hidden gorge, where water flowed freely, an oasis concealed by deceit, the drought a mirage, a cruel trick played by the creatures of the waterside.
