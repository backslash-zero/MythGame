Blinded by fear, villagers turned on frogs and snakes, blades flashing in the dry air. As the last creature fell, the world shimmered. The drought vanished, revealing a terrible truth. For their cruelty, the villagers transformed, skin turning scaled or slick, limbs shrinking or lengthening. They slithered and hopped through the village, a chorus of croaks and hisses echoing their regret.
