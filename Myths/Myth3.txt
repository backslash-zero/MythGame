In the village, where rain had not fallen for moons, frogs and snakes were blamed. "Kill them," the villagers cried, "they bring this drought!" Blades flashed, and blood flowed like the rivers that once were. Yet, as the last creature lay still, the air shimmered. A mirage lifted, revealing lush fields and full wells. Too late, they saw the truth: the drought was but an illusion, their violence a tragic dance in a lie.
