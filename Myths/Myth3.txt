As the hut filled with water, shimmering and strange, the men laughed, catching fish and fowl in the swirling chaos. The croak of frogs echoed, a chorus unnoticed. When the water receded, their prizes squirmed, transforming in their hands. Fish and fowl became flesh, their own flesh, wriggling and alive. They stared, horror-struck, as the frogs watched, their eyes gleaming with an ancient, amphibious secret.
